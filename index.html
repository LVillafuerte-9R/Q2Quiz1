Top 5 Philippine Historical Figures

Photo Name Description Contributions

Juan Luna

 Juan Luna is a Filipino painter and sculptor during the late 19th Century. He was born October 23, 1857 and died in Hong Kong at December 7. 1899. His cause of death was cardiac arrest or in short, heart attack. His greatest contribution was when he created the Spoliarium. The painting indicates that Juan Luna's strong desire of nationalism, justice, and freedom for the fellow Filipinos. Because of that, he won a gold medal at the National Exposition of Fine Arts in 1884.

Marcelo H. Del Pilar

 Marcelo H. Del Pilar is born on August 30, 1850 in Bulacan and died at July 4, 1896 in Barcelona Spain. His Cause of death was Tuberculosis. He was known for his intelligence and bravery and because of that, he made the Diariong Tagalog. The first language that Filipinos can use instead of Spanish.

Jose Rizal

 Jose Rizal is a Filipino writer who was born on June 19, 1861 and was executed on December 30, 1896. His greates contributions was when he wrote Noli Me Tangere. A story with chapters that tell us the struggle of Filipinos during his time.

Gabriela Silang

 Gabriela Silang was born on March 19, 1731 and died at September 20, 1763. She was the wife of Diego Silang, the leader of their village. She was known for avenging her husband. Her husband died because he was betrayed by one of his own friends. Because of that, she led the army as a way of avenging her husband. Eventually they won the war and Gabriela Silang was known for her bravery and courage.

Melchora Aquino

 Melchora Aquino is a Filipino revolutianry and was known as "Tandang Sora." She was born on january 6, 1812 and died at February 19, 1919. She was a member of the secret organization, the Katipunan. Her contributions were giving medical attention and saying words of encouragement to the Katipuneros to help them revolt against the Spaniards.
